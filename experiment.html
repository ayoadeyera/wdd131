Abuja, Nigeria

Abuja is the capital city of Nigeria, located in the center of the country. It was chosen as the capital in 1991 due to its central location, which makes it more accessible to people from all parts of Nigeria. Abuja is known for its modern architecture, including the Nigerian National Mosque and the Nigerian National Christian Centre. The city is also home to many government buildings, embassies, and international organizations. Abuja is a planned city with a mix of residential, commercial, and recreational areas, making it a vibrant and diverse place to live and work.
