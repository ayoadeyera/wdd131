Abuja, Nigeria

Abuja, the capital of Nigeria, sits proudly at the heart of the country.
Designated as the capital in 1991 for its central location, Abuja was chosen to promote unity and accessibility for all Nigerians, regardless of region. The city is renowned for its striking modern architecture, including iconic landmarks like the Nigerian National Mosque and the National Christian Centre.
As a purpose-built city, Abuja blends government institutions, embassies, and international organizations with thoughtfully planned residential, commercial, and recreational zones. This balance makes it not only the political center of Nigeria but also a vibrant, diverse, and dynamic place to live, work, and explore.
